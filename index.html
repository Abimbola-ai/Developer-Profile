Wind and Solar Farm Output Prediction

Application Development Coursework.

This project predicts the output power for a client that has both solar and wind power plants. Both these farms feed their output electrical energy to the local grid where they are located. The aim is to produce an application that is able to predict the amount of power we expect to generate on a day in the near future in order to provide this information to the grid operator. The information to be used to generate this model will include: Weather conditions (sunshine, wind speed, cloud cover etc) obtained from a weather API. Maintenance schedules (CSV files uploads in the application) can be uploaded. The effect of the weather on the electrical energy outputs was determined by looking at the historical data. During maintenance, some part of the plant is down. Therefore, when 30% of the plant is undergoing maintenance, for instance, the maximum capacity it can generate with ideal weather conditions is only 70% of its actual maximum.!

  • Date: September 2020
  • Category: Application Development
  • Tools: Python, Flask, Machine Learning, html, CSS

Harddrive Failure Prediction

Machine Learning Coursework.

The hard disk data includes basic drive information along with the S.M.A.R.T. statistics reported by each drive. The daily snapshot of one drive is one record or row of data. We have data from the period covering January to December 2016. When a drive fails, the 'failure' column is set to 1 on the day of failure, and starting the day after, the drive will be removed from the dataset. Each day, new drives are also added. This means that total number of drives each day may vary. The aim of the project was to fit machine learning models to predict if a drive will fail. Using this model, we will then be asked to make some recommendations for maintaining data integrity in an avionics computing system!

  • Date: June 2020
  • Category: Machine Learning
  • Tools: Python, Machine Learning

Oil/Gas Pipeline Corrosion Detection

Digital Twin.

Pipelines are used to transport both crude oil and gas to various destinations and in the course of transporting these fluids the possibility of the pipelines getting corroded due to wear and tear experienced from the fluid friction and other attributes exists. If not spotted early enough, corrosion can lead to pipeline fatiguing and, in worst-case scenarios, leaking of which, the results can be devastating. With this in mind, we thought it wise to embark on eliminating or at the barest minimum reducing the possibility of having pipelines corrode and leak harmful fluids to the environment by creating a digital twin which when modeled will inform us when there is a possibility of having a section of pipeline leak due to corrosion. This development will include corrosion prediction using machine-learning algorithms based on historical inspection data. The ‘digital twin’ model will allow operators to be able to access a virtual image of a pipeline at any point throughout its lifecycle.!

  • Date: November 2020
  • Category: Digital Twin
  • Tools: Python, Flask, Machine Learning, Blender
